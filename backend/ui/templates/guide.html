Overview

BATUHAN is an AI-powered system that automatically generates ISO audit reports. You provide your company documents, sample audit reports for style reference, and a blank Word template. The AI extracts evidence, writes the report, validates it, and delivers a final .docx file ready for review.

The process runs three AI stages — Evidence Extraction, Report Generation, and Validation — and typically completes in 10–15 minutes.

1. Company Documents

Minimum: 1 file. More is always better. These are your management system documents. The AI reads all of them to extract evidence. More relevant content = more accurate and detailed report.

What to include:

  • Quality / Environmental / Safety manual or policy
  • Procedures and work instructions
  • Risk registers and assessments
  • Training records and competency matrices
  • Internal audit reports and corrective actions
  • Management review minutes and KPI reports
  • Org charts, supplier evaluations, monitoring data

Accepted formats:

PDF · Word (.docx / .doc) · Plain text (.txt) · Scanned images (PNG, JPG)

⚠ The system skips unreadable files. If all files are unreadable, the job fails. Upload at least one clearly readable document.

2. Sample Audit Reports

Minimum: 1 file. Recommended: 2–3 files. Completed ISO audit reports from any auditor, for any company. Used only for writing style and tone. The system automatically blocks all company names and specific data from appearing in your report.

What makes a good sample:

  • Real completed audit reports in your preferred writing style
  • Ideally for the same ISO standard (but not required)
  • Multiple samples improve consistency of tone
⚠ Do not use the same company's documents as both a company document and a sample report. Sample reports are for style only — client data protection is built in.

3. Blank Report Template

Exactly 1 .docx file required. The structural skeleton of your report. The AI fills content under each section heading while preserving your exact structure and section titles.

Critical: sections MUST use Word Heading styles

Each section MUST use Word's built-in Heading 1, 2, or 3 style — not just bold or large text. The system identifies sections by style, not appearance.

How to apply Heading styles in Word:

  • Click on a section title in your document
  • In the Home tab → Styles panel → click Heading 1 (or 2/3)
  • Use View → Navigation Pane to confirm headings appear in the outline

Typical sections for an audit report:

  • Audit Scope & Objectives · Audit Team & Methodology
  • Document Review Summary · Audit Findings by Clause
  • Nonconformities · Opportunities for Improvement · Audit Conclusion
⚠ If the template has no Heading-style sections, the job fails immediately at preprocessing. Always verify headings are correctly applied before uploading.

Queued

~30 seconds

Step A — Evidence Extraction

AI reads all company documents and extracts structured evidence: company overview, processes, records, and identified gaps.

~2–4 minutes

Using extracted evidence, template structure, and style guidance, the AI writes the full audit report section by section.

~3–5 minutes

~2–4 minutes

~30 seconds

Final .docx report and correction log ready to download from the Results page.

6. Timing

A typical job takes 10–15 minutes end to end. Jobs with many large documents may take slightly longer due to text extraction time. The status page auto-refreshes every 4 seconds and redirects automatically when ready.

You can bookmark the status page URL and check back later — the report is stored and available until job data is cleared.

7. Downloading Your Results

Once complete you are redirected to the Results page. Two files are available:

  • Final Report (.docx) — complete audit report formatted using your template. Open in Microsoft Word or Google Docs.
  • Correction Log (.txt) — record of all changes the AI made in Step C. Useful for quality review.

Save both files immediately. Job data is not guaranteed to persist indefinitely.

8. Tips for Best Results

  • More documents = better report. Upload all available management system documentation, even if some sections feel redundant.
  • Use text-based PDFs. Scanned images rely on OCR which is slower and less accurate. Use native PDF or Word files where possible.
  • Match your template to the standard. Clause-specific sections (e.g. Clause 6 Planning, Clause 8 Operation) produce a more structured report.
  • Check your template headings. Use View → Navigation Pane in Word to confirm headings appear in the document outline.
  • Use 2–3 sample reports. More samples = better style consistency. Mix Stage 1 and Stage 2 samples if available.
